When the Government Closes: Economic Responses to Federal Shutdowns

Author: Phat Diep
Institution: Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar

This paper examines the immediate economic effects of U.S. federal government shutdowns using high-frequency data from the Weekly Economic Index (WEI), applying an event-study methodology to show that shutdowns cause a statistically significant short-term decline in real economic activity, with the largest impact occurring in the first full week after shutdown onset.
